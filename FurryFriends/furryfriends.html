My cutest friends Puss and Amber 💗

Puss

My cat Puss, a British Shorthair cat, is 4 and half years old already. He likes to sit in the sunshine to clean his hair, or sit in the cat tree to take a look at people and cars outside of the window. Every morning, he will jump on the bed and come to us purring.

When he was young, the vet highly suspected him having Feline infectious peritonitis, which is a heartbreaking disease and usually strikes kittens and is almost always fatal. Except in rare cases, wet FIP is fatal within about five weeks of diagnosis. The dry form is equally deadly but affected cats may survive for a few months. Me and my husband took him to several doctors and did many examinations. There is no drug to treat FIP, so the vet can only treat symptoms. It was like a miracle that he finally recovered. But he was timid and doesn't like most strangers or too many environmental changes from that time on. But for me, he is and will always be the cutest guy in the world!

These days he has reached 14 pounds and sometimes he cannot "brake" when he is playing games. Both me and the vet think he needs to lose some weight🙄.

Amber

Amber is a new member of our family from a few months ago! She is a standard size GoldenDoodle with cream wavy hair. She is only 6 months, still a puppy, and is REALLY energetic!

The most difficult part for me is to introduce Amber and Puss to each other. Amber always wants to play and Puss always feels scared and wants to hide because of the difference in their sizes and weight. To solve this issue, I even took an online class about training dogs.
